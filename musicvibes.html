Jaka Juhant imagines how to escape the  "the grind never stops"  lifestyle and invites us to a relaxing, meditative environment where we can immerse ourselves in the music and put our work on hold for a moment.

In the virtual environment in which we have landed after falling off a jet ski, we are surrounded by water lilies, various scattered objects and an iPod with a nostalgic playlist that takes us back to the seemingly carefree days of summer EDM music festivals. The artist also refers to the more recent past, the 2010s, by using tumblr aesthetics, motivational quotes, memes and other references of internet culture at the time, evoking a period of coming together in various internet communities, united by niche interests and less driven by algorithms than today.

Jaka Juhant's work often focuses on the experience of physicality in digital environments. In the work  Music Vibes  he looks nostalgically back to a musical era of the recent past, highlighting music's ability to trigger feelings of disembodied, transcendental states, evoking certain haunting memories and an escapist longing for simpler times.
